---
title: Touch some grass or how to survive during AI times
date:  2026-04-12
---

After sending too many cursed memes on Instagram to a friend, she said, “pls touch some grass”. And it’s true, I need to stop doomscrolling, and I think this is a valid suggestion also for surviving during AI, since in the software fields, we are too much bound to social networks. Sometimes we force ourselves to use socials in order to be updated, to check the last trends, new libraries, and it is useful, but on the other hand, the price to pay is high: time and attention span. Another social media’s issue that at some point I felt influenced by was this sort of psychosis bubbles: like AI will replace us all, or with AI, most engineers become 10x engineers. And maybe AI itself is not the problem, not even our skills as engineers, but it’s more related to the way we live and approach social media.

So here I will enounce a personal decalogue to survive during AI times and worry less about these bubbles:

1. Touch some grass and something manual/analogic
    Go outside, do something that doesn’t involve watching a screen for too long: in my case I will try to paint, listen to music, read books, see friends, relax. This will improve a bit some mental health (feeling less worried about AI fatigues, last LLM updates, etc.), and physical health too (like doing some sports, save some eyes from screens or displays).

2. Try things yourself
    Be curious, use AI and form an opinion on it by yourself. 

3. Focus on one thing at a time
    Social network destroys attention spans. Working, watching social networks or listening to music, podcasts contemporaneously, it’s just the incapacity to focus and doing one thing well as [Fellini said](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pSgOwDAhHtI) during the '80s.

4. [Pessimism of the intellect, optimism of the will](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pessimismo_dell%27intelligenza,_ottimismo_della_volont%C3%A0)
    These times are challenging and they seem harder than ever: not only in our field there are constant changes, but all the world balance is shifting rapidly; it is what it is, [embrace the world changes like the changing seasons](https://www.devas.life/what-a-japanese-cooking-principle-taught-me-about-overcoming-ai-fatigue/?utm_media=youtube). On the other side, we don't need to despair, we can do something for changing the world.

5. Dedicate precise time to social networks/news
    Or try to count or think about the amount of time you spend on socials, try to mitigate that doomscrolling sensation in any way.

6. Do what you want to do
    As Seneca said, life is not too short, we waste a lot of it. I don’t want to waste my time on social networks or thinking what social networks say. Meanwhile, I’m sure I will always continue to code and create my own things even if AI will make me lose my job or the coding part will be less and less important, because that’s what I want to do. Set a prize on your time: that is the difference between existing and living.

7. Become who you are
    It’s not easy to become yourself and stay well with yourself.

8. Fuck
    Maybe this would be the first point, also drink some coffee.

9. Make friends
    I’m in a period where all my friends are far from me and I feel it, in a place where everyone think about only the job and nothing else, I think it’s worth to try to make friends.

10. Choose your own 10th point.


Of course this is a personal reminder for me, in the future there will be times when I will re-read this and maybe this will help!
